Data Processing: Imputation

  • Accelerometer data can be missing for many reasons (power on/off, signal, airplane mode, erratic participant behavior)

  • Assumptions:

    • Missing accelerometer data, and it’s night time when you usually don’t -> “dormancy”
    • Missing accelerometer data, and it’s a time that you usually do -> “missing data” that should be imputed
    • Missing accelerometer data, but you have no screen on-off events -> “dormancy”

  • Used a Hidden Markov Model to impute this data

Mixed Models + Markov Chains Allow You to Consider Repeating Patterns

if a person tends to be active or at rest during the same hours of the day routinely, then the restful hours will have very low random intercepts, with active hours having high random intercepts

By accounting for these mixed effects, they hope to get a better idea of activity rhythms (ARM) measured by accelerometer and screen events, moderated by the likelihood of changing activity

ARM as an Outcome in Linear Regression for Sleep

ARM ~ Mean Hours of Sleep + Bedtime + Wake up time + PSQI

📈ARM = “stronger routines” = 📉 variance in time-to-sleep/wake + 📈sleep duration

ARM as an Outcome in Association with Depression Measures

ARM ~ BDI_x

📈ARM = “stronger routines” = 📉 depression domains in the Beck Depression Index

Conclusions

  • “Regularity of routine” (ARM) quantified using accelerometer and screen on-off
  • CT-HMM modeled ARM accounting for the “hour-of-the-day effect”
  • ARM associated with sleep, mood self-report
  • Limited sample size –> non-significant p-values

  • ARM associated with demographics, but not sleep, mood inventories

  • Reflects that diarised methods associated with sleep quality and demographics

  • ARM only accounts for activity
